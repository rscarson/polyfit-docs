What is Polyfit?

Polyfit is a high-performance, easy-to-use polynomial fitting library for Rust, designed to make curve fitting accessible to software engineers without a background in statistics.

Why Polyfit?

Polyfit is designed to make curve fitting easy and accessible, without requiring a deep understanding of the underlying mathematics - Built by a software engineer for software engineers.

Performance Focused

Optimized for speed and memory efficiency

✓ Blazing fast on both large and small datasets.

✓ Handles 100 million-row Chebyshev fits in ~1 s (8 cores, 2.2 GHz).

Polyfit is generic over the choice of basis, and handles the domains and details, giving you the flexibility to choose the best fit for your data, and to seamlessly switch basis as needed.

Monomial

The classic "x^n" basis.
Flexible, universal, and easy to read.

Chebyshev

Stable, fast and, efficient.
Orthogonal and resistant to overfitting.

Legendre

Orthogonal and numerically stable.
Good for approximating functions over finite intervals.

Laguerre

Orthogonal over [0, ∞).
Useful for modeling decay processes.

Physicist's Hermite

Orthogonal, used in physics and probability theory.
Tuned for use in quantum mechanics.

Probabilist's Hermite

Orthogonal, used in probability theory.
Tuned for use in statistics.

Fourier

Stable, orthogonal, and periodic.
Ideal for modeling cyclical data or signals.

Logarithmic

Useful for modeling data with exponential growth or decay.
